# Distribution and Content Multiplication Strategy

## Primary Channels

1. **YouTube** — Full video episodes + short clips. YouTube is the discovery engine. SEO-optimized titles targeting staffing pain points ("How to Screen 200 Resumes in 10 Minutes", "Why Your ATS Is Filtering Out Good Candidates").
2. **Spotify** — Audio-only feed. Second largest podcast platform. Staffing professionals listen during commutes and between calls.
3. **Apple Podcasts** — Standard distribution. Important for credibility and discoverability.
4. **LinkedIn** — PRIMARY social channel. This is where staffing professionals live. Every episode produces content here.

## LinkedIn Content Strategy (Most Important)

For each episode, produce:

- **1 Long-form post** (300-500 words) — The key insight from the episode, written as a standalone story
- **3-5 Short video clips** (60-90 seconds) — The most impactful moments, captioned, vertical format
- **1 Carousel** (5-8 slides) — Key data points and takeaways in visual format
- **1 Poll** — Related to the episode topic ("What's your agency's biggest AI challenge?")
- **2-3 Quote cards** — Pullable quotes from the guest or from Justin
- Stagger across the week: Post day 1 (episode drop), day 2 (clip), day 3 (carousel), day 4 (clip), day 5 (poll)

## Show Notes to GitHub Repo Integration

Every episode's show notes link directly to the relevant files in the public repo:

- "We discussed resume screening" -> link to `solutions/automation-blueprints/resume-screening-agent.md`
- "Our guest runs a healthcare agency" -> link to `solutions/by-agency-type/healthcare-staffing.md`
- "The 67 problems we mapped" -> link to `docs/research/problems-and-automation-guide.md`

This drives traffic to the repo, which IS the proof of depth and the soft conversion mechanism.

## Newsletter Integration

- Weekly email tied to episode releases
- Exclusive "behind the scenes" content not in the episode
- Link to the episode + 3 key takeaways + repo resource of the week
- Build email list from: LinkedIn CTA, show notes CTA, repo visitors, guest networks

## Growth Strategy

- **Month 1-3:** Foundation. 12 episodes. Build consistent publishing cadence. Guest network effect (each guest shares to their audience).
- **Month 4-6:** Amplification. Cross-promote with staffing industry events (ASA, SIA). Guest appearances on other podcasts. LinkedIn engagement compounds.
- **Month 7-12:** Authority. Recognized as THE AI-in-staffing voice. Speaking invitations. Partnership opportunities. Inbound dominates outbound.

## Metrics to Track

- Downloads per episode (target: 500+ by episode 12)
- LinkedIn engagement (impressions, comments, shares per post)
- GitHub repo traffic from show notes links
- Newsletter subscriber growth
- Guest referral rate (% of guests who refer other guests)
- Inbound inquiries attributed to podcast content
